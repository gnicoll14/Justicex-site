Recitals

Dana R. Whitfield (“Dana”) and Samuel T. Whitfield (“Samuel”), married on June 14, 2014 and residing in Nassau County, New York, have two children: Emma (age 9) and Mason (age 6). The parties participated voluntarily in a structured, symmetric process in which each side entered its own information privately, exchanged financial disclosure through informed, affirmative releases, and reviewed the same objective comparison of the resulting record. Each party had the opportunity to consult independent counsel at every step and acknowledges the other's right to do the same. This Memorandum records the terms the parties themselves agreed to. It is intended for review by each party's attorney and takes legal effect only as the parties and their counsel determine.

Article 1 · Parenting

The parties agree to joint legal custody of Emma and Mason, with the children residing primarily with Dana. Samuel will have parenting time on alternating weekends (Friday evening through Sunday evening), one midweek evening each week, and shared holiday and school-break time as set out in the parties' agreed Parenting Plan of even date (synthetic sample). Both parents will share major decisions on health, education, and general welfare, and each will keep the other informed of matters affecting the children.

Article 2 · Child support

During the process, the parties reviewed the New York child support guideline computations together, side by side, on the same figures. By agreement, Samuel will pay Dana basic child support of $2,150 per month, beginning August 1, 2026. The parties will share unreimbursed medical expenses and work-related childcare 70% (Samuel) / 30% (Dana), approximately in proportion to their agreed income basis (Samuel $160,000; Dana $70,000)4. The parties will exchange updated income information annually and will revisit support on a material change in circumstances or as the law provides. [Illustrative figures — synthetic sample.]

Article 3 · Spousal maintenance

By agreement, and after reviewing the guideline computations together, Samuel will pay Dana maintenance of $1,500 per month for 60 months, beginning August 1, 2026. Maintenance ends earlier on the death of either party, Dana's remarriage, or the parties' further written agreement. [Illustrative figures — synthetic sample.]

Article 4 · Equitable distribution

The parties reviewed the account records each side provided and agree on the classification of every asset. In particular, based on the retirement account statements Samuel provided and Dana reviewed1, the parties agree that $70,000 of Samuel's 401(k) balance is his separate property (his pre-marital balance and its growth) and $240,000 is marital. The marital estate is divided 50 / 50 on a net-of-tax basis, as set out in Exhibit A, which reconciles to the parties' agreed net marital estate of $555,200.

  1. Dana retains the marital residence2 and its equity, and will refinance the mortgage into her name within nine months. From the refinance proceeds she will pay Samuel a distributive payment of $30,960. If the refinance cannot be completed in that window, the parties will list the residence for sale and divide the net proceeds per Exhibit A.
  2. Samuel retains his 401(k) (both the agreed separate portion and the marital portion credited to his column in Exhibit A). Dana retains her 403(b).
  3. Samuel retains the joint brokerage account3 and the joint savings; the two vehicles remain with their current drivers as shown in Exhibit A.

Article 5 · Debts

The parties have no material joint debts other than the mortgage addressed above. Each party retains and is solely responsible for the accounts and cards in that party's own name, and will hold the other harmless from them.

Article 6 · Insurance and benefits

The children remain covered under Samuel's employer health plan while eligible; the parties share premiums attributable to the children and unreimbursed costs per Article 2. While support obligations run, Samuel will maintain term life insurance of $500,000 and Dana of $250,000, each naming the other as trustee for the benefit of the children.

Article 7 · Taxes

The parties will alternate claiming the children for tax purposes — Dana in odd years, Samuel in even years — subject to the eligibility rules they reviewed together, and will cooperate in signing any forms needed to give effect to this arrangement. Each party remains responsible for that party's own tax filings and may confirm individual treatment with a tax professional of that party's choosing.

Article 8 · The disclosure record

Each party exchanged financial disclosure through the informed, affirmative releases recorded in the matter. The documents each side provided, and the figures drawn from them, are cited throughout this Memorandum and preserved in the certified matter record (Exhibit B). Each party confirms the disclosure that party provided is complete and accurate to that party's knowledge.

Article 9 · Future changes

If circumstances change, the parties will first exchange the relevant information and attempt to agree on an adjustment — using the same process that produced this Memorandum if they choose. Nothing in this Memorandum limits either party's right to seek any relief a court may provide.

Article 10 · Independent review and intended use

Each party acknowledges: this Memorandum records the parties' own agreed terms; it was prepared through software that organizes and compares information and does not give legal advice; each party has had, and continues to have, the right to independent counsel; and the parties intend to submit this Memorandum for their attorneys' review and to execute it, and any further documents, as they and their counsel determine.
