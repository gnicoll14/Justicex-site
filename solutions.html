01 The Pipeline

Seven stages.
Three checkpoints.

The platform runs the matter end-to-end. You sign off at three human-in-the-loop checkpoints — after redaction, after the evidence-quality rating, and before the joint session. Every other stage is deterministic, symmetric, and audit-logged.

01 Plan
Define the matter scope, lock the specific issues to resolve, and select the resolution path. Both parties get identical scope documents.
02 Intake
Each party uploads evidence and artifacts; the system structures submissions and flags quality and completeness. Symmetric prompts — neither party sees the other's.
AES-256 at rest TLS 1.3 in transit
03 Redact
Automated identification and removal of PII and privileged content before any cross-party sharing — for privacy and compliance.
Checkpoint 1 · Post-redaction
Human-in-the-loop: counsel reviews a side-by-side diff of removed content and signs off on the redaction certificate.
04 Summarize
Symmetric position briefs for both parties, generated with identical prompts and models to eliminate prep-quality advantages. Citation-grounded to source.
05 Gap Analysis
Objective comparison on a shared rubric; a matter-specific issue tree tags disputed vs. undisputed points and surfaces the gaps between the parties.
Checkpoint 2 · Post-comparison
Human-in-the-loop: the practitioner confirms the comparison rationale and can make claim-level overrides, with the rationale captured.
Checkpoint 3 · Pre-resolution
Human-in-the-loop: a final review of the gap analysis and alignment before entering the joint session.
06 Resolution
An objective option set and points of alignment, based on the symmetric comparison and identified gaps. JusticeX informs; the parties decide.
07 Memorandum
Assembles a final, audit-traceable Memorandum of Understanding grounded in the parties' agreed facts.
Cryptographic audit ledger
Encrypted end-to-end. AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit.
Audit-traceable. Every output is linked to the source records it was generated from, via a cryptographic audit ledger.
Symmetric processing. Same prompts, same models, both sides — the prep-quality advantage engineered out.
02 Deliverables

What the platform produces.

Every output is citation-grounded, deterministically generated, and reconciled to the SPOC. Nothing leaves the matter without a counsel-readable trail back to its source.

D1

Position briefs

Each party's claims, supporting facts, and requested remedies — symmetrically structured, source-anchored, redaction-aware.

  • Avg generation · 4 min
  • Citations per brief · 20–60
  • Source anchors · Mandatory
D2

Claim evidence-quality ratings

Symmetric assessment of completeness and evidence quality on one shared rubric — the same for both sides; deltas surfaced for the mediator. Not a legal-merit or outcome score.

  • Rubric · Calibrated · auditable
  • Jurisdiction · NY (Phase 1)
  • Symmetry check · Deterministic
D3

Gap-analysis map

Objective option space, visualized. Concession curves, objection clusters, and points of alignment.

  • Updates · Live
  • Export · PDF · CSV · JSON
  • Privilege · Mediator-only
D4

Memorandum of Understanding

Draft agreement with clause-level citations to intake, statute, and prior comparable matters. Counsel-readable. Ready for counsel review and e-signature on sign-off.

  • Format · Counsel-grade
  • Signature · Mediator + parties
  • Cert · Cryptographic
D5

Audit ledger

Every prompt, every model output, every checkpoint sign-off — hash-chained, exportable, defensible under counsel review.

  • Retention · 7 years default
  • Export · JSON · PDF
  • Hashing · SHA-256 chain
D6

Neutrality report

Per-matter symmetry audit. Deterministic check that both parties received the same prompts, scoring, and access at every stage.

  • Run cadence · Per output
  • Public version · Annual aggregate
  • Failure mode · Block + escalate
