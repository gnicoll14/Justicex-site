Step 1 · Build your network

How to use this page

  1. Import your connections. Drop your LinkedIn Connections.csv below — it's read and scored on your own device, never uploaded.?
  2. Review your ranked list. Your contacts sort into tiers — Tier 1 = call first (senior + best fit).?
  3. Pick who to pursue. Decide which contacts are worth a real conversation. Only these move forward — everyone else stays private to you.
  4. Move them to your pipeline. Add each chosen contact to Step 3 · Convert your network, then run the play on them.
Working tool · Build your plan

Turn your network into a ranked call list — privately.

Load your LinkedIn connections and this tool profiles, scores, and tiers them, so your warmest, best-fit accounts rise to the top — the same method that turns a whole network into a focused pursue list.

🔒

Your contacts are never shared with JusticeX. The file is read and scored entirely on your own device — nothing is uploaded. A person becomes visible to JusticeX only if you move that specific contact into a real pipeline opportunity. Everyone you don't pursue stays yours alone.

What file — and how to get it

A .csv is a plain spreadsheet file (it opens in Excel or Google Sheets). LinkedIn gives you one that simply lists your connections' names, companies, and titles.

  1. On LinkedIn: Settings & Privacy → Data Privacy → Get a copy of your data → Connections.
  2. Download the file (it's usually named Connections.csv).
  3. Drop it above. Your ranked plan appears instantly — only on your screen.
“If I drop my real connections here, are they still private?” Yes. The file is processed in your browser and is never sent anywhere — not to JusticeX, not to your team, not to anyone. Close the tab and it's gone. You choose, one at a time, which contacts ever become opportunities.

Step 2 · Engage your network

How to use this page

  1. Start with your Tier-1 accounts. Work the five touches below in order, one account at a time.
  2. Reconnect first — don't pitch. Touch 1 is a short personal note that re-opens the relationship.
  3. Earn the conversation, then share. Touches 2–3: listen for the gap, then send the offering.
  4. Log every touch as you go. Update the deal's stage in Step 3 · Convert your network so your forecast stays live.

Five touches, warm intro to signed.

A warm sale is a few high-quality touches, not a drip. Run this for each Tier-1 account.

Reconnect — don't pitch

A short personal note that re-opens the relationship. Aim for a yes to a quick call.

Earn a 20-minute conversation

Listen for the dispute-cost or benefit gap — that's the qualification, not a demo.

Share the offering

Send the one-pager and a short overview once you understand their world.

Scope a design-partner pilot

Name the champion, map the approval step, agree the pilot shape.

Propose & close

Founding-partner terms out, verbal commit, then signed — and your commission lands.

Step 3 · Convert your network

How to use this page

  1. Add the people you're pursuing first. Click + Add from my network to pull a ranked contact from your plan into the table below. This is what fills your pipeline — do it before anything else.
  2. Advance each deal. As a conversation moves forward, update its stage — your weighted value and commission recalculate automatically.?
  3. Register to protect the account. Use Register a deal (top right) so the account is credited to you.?
  4. Work offline if you like. Download the pipeline & earnings tracker gives you the full forecast math in Excel. Optional — the page already tracks everything for you.

Step 4 · Track your progress

How to read this page

  1. Start at attainment. The top bar shows how close you are to your annual target and whether you're on pace.?

  3. Use your 1:1 talking points. Scroll to the auto-drafted points and walk them top-to-bottom in your manager check-in.?
