Legal & Unauthorized-Practice-of-Law Disclaimer

JusticeX.ai is a software platform. It provides objective, mathematical analysis only. It is not a law firm, mediator, or arbitrator, and it does not give legal advice.

What JusticeX is

JusticeX.ai is a Software-as-a-Service platform — not a law firm, mediator, arbitrator, or legal-services provider. It does not practice law, give legal advice, render opinions, or represent anyone before a court, agency, or tribunal. Using it creates no attorney-client, mediator-party, or fiduciary relationship.

What the platform does — two objective options

Default
Objective comparison. A symmetric, side-by-side comparison of the information submitted by Party A and Party B — the same prompts, the same models, the same depth for both parties.
Optional overlay
Objective legal precedent. Cited statutes, regulations, and case law from public sources, surfaced alongside the comparison for reference only.

You choose either option, or both, per matter. Both outputs are mathematically extrapolated from the parties' own submissions and public legal authority — objective and quantitative, never subjective.

What the platform does not do

JusticeX.ai does not interpret law, weigh legal judgments, recommend or shape arguments, advise on strategy, or predict outcomes. Precedent is shown for reference only — without interpretation or opinion on outcome. Interpretive and strategic decisions stay with you and your attorney, mediator, or neutral; the resolution is the parties' own. JusticeX may flag objective inconsistencies in a party's inputs — figures that don't reconcile, or an entry that contradicts that party's own document — to inform evidence quality; it never determines truthfulness, credibility, or intent. Public legal materials are shown for reference only, may be incomplete or superseded, and the law changes over time.

Unauthorized practice of law

JusticeX.ai does not act as your attorney, lawyer, counsel, or legal representative, and does not engage in the practice of law. It does not represent any party before any court, agency, arbitrator, or tribunal; does not select, recommend, draft, or advise on legal strategy, arguments, claims, or defenses; does not apply law to your facts; and does not exercise the professional legal judgment a licensed attorney provides. Using JusticeX.ai creates no attorney-client relationship, and no communication with or through the platform is privileged unless an independently retained attorney establishes that privilege. Only a lawyer licensed in your jurisdiction can advise you on your legal rights and obligations.

AI use and risks

JusticeX uses AI — large language models, including Anthropic's Claude — to summarize submissions, compare the parties' positions and values, score completeness and objective strength (the same way for both sides), flag gaps against the other party or a legal standard, research public legal precedent when selected, and draft documents for review. Every AI-assisted screen is labeled.

AI can be wrong — errors, omissions, bias, or fabricated content ("hallucinations"), including bad or non-existent citations. It is not legal advice and predicts no outcome. Treat each output as a draft: verify any cited authority, and have a licensed attorney or neutral review it before you rely on, send, or file it.

Calculations, models, and your circumstances

JusticeX is software. Like any software — and like any financial model, formula, or calculation — its outputs can contain mathematical, model, or logical errors and can rest on assumptions that don't hold in a given matter. Figures, comparisons, scores, valuations, net-of-tax and present-value calculations, and projections are estimates, not verified results. Every matter is different: a method right for one user, dispute, or jurisdiction may be wrong for another, and JusticeX does not determine whether a given calculation fits your facts. Review, test, correct, and update every output before you rely on, send, or file it — you are responsible for any output you adopt.

Information you provide

The comparisons, scores, and other outputs come entirely from what each party submits. JusticeX does not independently verify the truthfulness, accuracy, completeness, or authorization of any submission, and any evidence-quality signal reflects objective indicators only — not a finding of truth, credibility, or intent. Outputs are only as reliable as the information entered.
