How your matter helps JusticeX improve

JusticeX gets better over time — but never by training AI on your documents. You control exactly what, if anything, is used beyond running your own matter. The conservative setting is on by default; the rest is opt-in and reversible anytime.

Run my matter Default · always on

Your content is used only to run your matter. Never used to train AI models, never shared. This is the baseline and can't be turned off — it's just how the service works.

Help improve the product Opt-in

Share de-identified quality signals — where the process stalled, where a figure didn't reconcile, whether an AI output needed correction. Metadata only; none of your documents or figures leave your matter.

Contribute a synthetic example Opt-in

Allow JusticeX to create a fully synthetic version of your matter — realistic but about no real person — for its example library and quality tests. Your actual documents are never published or shared; only the synthetic derivative is kept, after a human privacy review. Reversible.

Preference saved

Both parties decide together. Anything drawn from the shared matter record (beyond your own private side) requires every party on the matter to opt in. One party can't contribute the shared record on the other's behalf. Declining never changes the service you get.

What JusticeX never does
Regardless of the settings above:
  • Your content is never used to train public or third-party AI models.
  • Your actual documents are never shown to other users — examples in the library are synthetic.
  • Redaction isn't treated as anonymization; a real matter is never "just redacted and shared."
  • You can withdraw an opt-in at any time.
How improvement actually works

Human corrections teach the process

When a professional edits or overrides an AI output, that judgment — de-identified, and only with your opt-in — sharpens the checks and prompts. The model's weights are never trained on your content.

A synthetic example library

Realistic examples for other users are generated as synthetic matters — no real person, ever — so you can learn from a comparable case without anyone's real file being exposed.
